Why are mosquitoes such a severe problem in coastal Chennai?

Chennai's proximity to the Bay of Bengal keeps humidity extremely high year-round. Combined with two monsoon seasons (southwest June–September and northeast October–December), stagnant water in low-lying areas, terraces and construction sites creates ideal breeding grounds for Aedes aegypti and Culex mosquitoes throughout the year.

Are your treatments effective for termites in Chennai's old buildings?

Yes. Chennai's older constructions in Mylapore, Adyar, Triplicane and Chromepet frequently have decades-old termite activity. We use both soil injection treatment and baiting systems suited to existing structures without requiring major excavation. All anti-termite work comes with a 5-year warranty.

Do you cover the OMR and Sholinganallur IT corridor?

Yes. We serve all OMR locations from Perungudi to Sholinganallur, Siruseri and beyond, including Thoraipakkam, Pallikaranai and Perungalathur. Same-day service available for OMR IT offices and residential complexes when you call before noon.

How often should Chennai homes get pest control treatment?

Due to Chennai's year-round pest pressure from coastal humidity, a quarterly AMC is strongly recommended for homes. Pre-monsoon and post-monsoon treatments are especially important for mosquito and cockroach control. Restaurants and food businesses should opt for monthly service to maintain FSSAI compliance.
